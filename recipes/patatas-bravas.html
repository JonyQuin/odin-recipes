patatas bravas

Description

Patatas bravas, or "fierce potatoes," is a classic Spanish tapas dish known for its bold, spicy sauce. This simple yet delicious dish consists of white potatoes cut into irregular cubes, fried in oil, and served with a spicy tomato sauce and sometimes aioli. The result is a perfect balance of crispy, tender potatoes with a flavorful kick.

The recipe

Ingredients:

For the potatoes:

  • 4 large white potatoes, peeled and cut into 2-3 cm cubes
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Salt, to taste

For the bravas sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to taste)
  • 1 can (400g) of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Optional for serving:

  • Chopped parsley, for garnish
  • Aioli

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes:
    • Rinse the cubed potatoes under cold water to remove any excess starch. Pat them dry with a towel.
    • Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the potatoes in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until they're golden and crispy on all sides. This should take about 10-15 minutes per batch.
    • Once fried, transfer the potatoes to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Sprinkle with a little salt while they're still warm.
  2. Make the Bravas Sauce:
    • In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it's soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
    • Add the minced garlic, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper to the onions. Cook for another minute, stirring constantly, until the spices are fragrant.
    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes and red wine vinegar. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then lower the heat and let it cook for about 20 minutes. The sauce should thicken slightly.
    • Once the sauce has thickened, taste and season with salt and pepper as needed. For a smoother sauce, you can blend it with an immersion blender or in a stand blender until it reaches your desired consistency.
  3. Serve:
    • To serve, place the fried potatoes on a serving plate. Pour the bravas sauce over the potatoes, or serve it on the side for dipping.
    • If using, drizzle with aioli and garnish with chopped parsley.
    • Serve immediately while the potatoes are still warm.
